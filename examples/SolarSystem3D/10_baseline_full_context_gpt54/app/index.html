Seasons and axial tilt

Earth's seasons are caused by axial tilt relative to sunlight, not by Earth being much closer to or farther from the Sun.

The spring equinox and autumn equinox place Earth where the axis is neither leaning strongly toward nor away from the Sun. The summer solstice tilts the Northern Hemisphere toward the Sun, and the winter solstice tilts it away.

This simulator uses simplified educational positions rather than precision ephemeris data.
