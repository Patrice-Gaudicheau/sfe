Why seasons happen

Seasons are caused by Earth’s axial tilt relative to sunlight, not by Earth being closer to or farther from the Sun. These presets use simplified teaching positions: at the equinoxes the axis is not leaning strongly toward or away from the Sun, while at the solstices the Northern Hemisphere tilts toward or away from sunlight.

Astronomy notes

This simulator uses simplified educational positions. Seasons are caused by Earth’s axial tilt relative to sunlight, not by Earth being closer to or farther from the Sun.

The spring equinox, summer solstice, autumn equinox, and winter solstice controls place Earth at simplified seasonal positions rather than precision ephemeris dates.

Educational scale is the initial default so Mercury through Neptune, Saturn’s rings, the Moon’s orbit, and body labels remain easy to inspect together.

Realistic scale better preserves relative distance and size relationships, but still keeps small bodies minimally visible so they do not disappear during navigation.

This app uses pinned browser ES module imports for Three.js and OrbitControls. If your browser blocks module loading from file://, run a simple static server as described in the README.

Interaction notes

  • OrbitControls provide rotate, zoom, and pan camera movement
  • Camera preset buttons offer overview, inner, Earth-and-Moon, outer, and Sun-focused framing
  • Clicking a body or activating a visible label updates selection and focuses the camera
  • Labels remain keyboard reachable when visible and leave the tab order when hidden
  • Core keyboard shortcuts: Space play/pause, L labels, O orbits, F refocus selected body, Arrow keys adjust speed, 1–5 camera presets
  • Shortcuts do not override normal typing behavior in standard form inputs and selects
  • No audio, music, narration, or generated sound is included in this static app
